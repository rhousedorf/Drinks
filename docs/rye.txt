        Picon Amer - David LebovitzThe best substitutes for Amer Picon, 
        a French bittersweet orange liqueur, 
        are Torani Amer (a popular US-based alternative) or Bigallet China-China. 
        Other excellent replacements include Amer Boudreau, Amaro Ciociaro with orange bitters, 
        or a mix of Ramazzotti and Cointreau. These options mimic the essential quinine-driven 
        bitter orange profile. 

        Top Substitutes for Picon Amer
        Torani Amer (Best Overall): Specifically designed to replace Picon in the 
        US (notably Nevada Basque Picon Punch).
        Bigallet China-China (Top Premium): A French alternative with similar 
        quinine, bitter orange, and herbal notes.
        Amer Boudreau: A DIY recipe known as the closest match to the original formula.
        Amaro Ciociaro: A good substitute with similar flavor, though slightly 
        less fruity, often paired with orange bitters. 

        DIY Substitution Recipes
        If you cannot find specialized substitutes, try these mixes:
        Simple Mix: 1 oz Amaro (Averna or Ramazzotti) + 
        1 oz Orange Liqueur (Cointreau or Pierre Ferrand Dry Curaçao).
        Amer Boudreau Mix: 2 parts Ramazzotti, 1 part Combier (orange liqueur), 
        plus dashes of orange and blood orange bitters. 

        Commonly Recommended Alternatives
        Ramazzotti Amaro: Good on its own for a milder, cola-like profile.
        Golden Moon Distillery Amer dit Picon: A craft US version, often used in Colorado.
        Cynar: A suitable bitter alternative for specific cocktails like a Brooklyn.
